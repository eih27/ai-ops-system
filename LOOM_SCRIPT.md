# Loom Demo Script
## AI Startup Operations System — 5-minute walkthrough

**Goal:** Show a real before/after transformation. Move fast. Let the output quality speak.

---

### [0:00 – 0:30] Hook — state the problem

> "Every startup meeting produces a pile of notes that someone has to turn into action items, stakeholder updates, and follow-up emails. That synthesis takes 45 to 90 minutes — every time. This is a system that does it in under 2 minutes."

*[Screen: show the app open, empty state. Point to the workflow map at the top.]*

> "The workflow is simple: messy input goes in, AI synthesis happens, structured execution outputs come out."

---

### [0:30 – 1:00] Show the input

> "Let me show you what messy really looks like."

*[Click "Load sample" with Sprint / Team Meeting selected.]*

> "This is a real-looking set of sprint sync notes. You can see: bullet points everywhere, mixed priorities, no clear owners, a few side conversations mixed in. This is what notes actually look like after a real meeting."

*[Scroll through the input briefly — don't read it all.]*

> "A senior PM or ops lead would take 45 minutes to turn this into something useful. Let me show you what happens instead."

---

### [1:00 – 1:30] Generate

*[Click "Generate Ops Pack." While the loader runs (about 2 seconds):]*

> "In production, this calls an AI model — Claude or GPT-4o — with a structured prompt that extracts decisions, priorities, and action items. Right now I'm using high-quality mock outputs to keep the demo reliable, but the architecture is fully API-ready."

*[Output appears. Tab bar is visible.]*

---

### [1:30 – 2:30] Walk the outputs — Summary + Action Items

*[Click Summary tab.]*

> "First, an executive summary. Two paragraphs. It captures the context, the key risks, and the overall sprint health — amber in this case. This is what you'd share at the start of a leadership review."

*[Click Action Items tab.]*

> "This is where I think the real value shows up. Every action item has an owner, a priority, a function, and a due timeframe. It's structured, scannable, and ready to paste directly into Linear or Jira. No reformatting needed."

*[Scroll through the table briefly.]*

> "Notice the priorities: P0 items like the API bug are flagged High in red. Medium items are amber. The color coding makes it immediately obvious where to focus."

---

### [2:30 – 3:15] PRD and Decisions

*[Click Decisions tab.]*

> "Next, key decisions — with status. Green means it's been decided. Amber means it's open and needs resolution. Red means it's P0. This is the difference between a meeting and an operations system: explicit decision tracking."

*[Click PRD tab.]*

> "The PRD draft is structured like a real product brief — problem statement, success metrics, MVP scope, out of scope, technical notes. One meeting note about a feature request becomes a shippable requirements doc."

---

### [3:15 – 4:00] Email and Stakeholder Update

*[Click Follow-up Email tab.]*

> "This is usually the output that gets the strongest reaction in demos. A complete follow-up email — subject line included — generated from bullet-point notes. I can copy it with one click and send it. That's about 20 minutes of work, gone."

*[Click Stakeholder Update tab.]*

> "The stakeholder update is designed for leadership: tight, factual, no fluff. It covers what happened, what's being resolved, and what decisions are still open. Ready to share as-is."

---

### [4:00 – 4:30] Impact Estimate + Workflow Map

*[Scroll down to Impact Estimate section.]*

> "At the bottom, the system shows you what it just saved: about 50 minutes of manual synthesis, 7 structured outputs generated, and a suggested next step — share the stakeholder update, then assign tickets."

*[Point to Workflow Map at the top.]*

> "The workflow map at the top summarizes the whole system: messy input, AI synthesis, structured outputs, team execution. That's the loop this system closes."

---

### [4:30 – 5:00] Closing — what's next

> "A few things I'd build next: real AI integration using Claude — the code is structured for it, it's a 30-minute swap — and one-click export to Notion or Linear."

> "The architecture is designed around the same pattern that underlies agentic workflows: structured input, AI synthesis, structured output. What makes this different from a generic AI wrapper is the operational framing — the system knows what a startup team needs at the end of a sprint sync, and it produces exactly that."

> "Thanks for watching. Happy to walk through the code or talk about how I'd extend this."

---

### Tips for recording

- Use a 1280×800 or 1440×900 window — fills the frame cleanly
- Load the Sprint Meeting sample (it has the most visible before/after contrast)
- Move through tabs at a moderate pace — let the content load fully before speaking about it
- Keep your cursor active to guide attention
- Don't narrate every field — let the output quality demonstrate itself

---

*Total runtime target: 4:45 – 5:15*
